About Me

Hey, I'm Henry. I'm currently a founding engineer at eXRealityAI and a CS grad student at UT Dallas. I did my BSc Honors at the University of Alberta.

At eXReality, I'm building agentic systems for mixed reality applications and for companies with large custom knowledge bases, and working on inference on edge devices (NVIDIA Jetson, Apple MLX). On the research side, I'm contributing to a project with Dr. Katherine Brown on ultrasound segmentation, applying distillation, quantization, and newer architectures like iJEPA. I'm pretty fortunate to make a living out of work I genuinely enjoy.

Like most Asian kids, I got into math pretty early, doing competitions and olympiad prep. The contest tricks were fun, but what really stuck was learning how to take a messy problem and find the right angle to crack it. That eventually led me to research at Amii, where I worked on online algorithms (using ML predictions to optimize for likely inputs without sacrificing worst-case guarantees) and multi-armed bandits (exploration-exploitation tradeoffs). Over time I got more drawn to the gap between theory and practice, actually building things that work on real hardware, which is what brought me to where I am now.

I write occasionally at henryvu.blog. Recent posts: Context Engineering for LLMs, Multi-Armed Bandits: Foundations.
